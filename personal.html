Being ECO Friendly when using personal care products.


THE BIG ISSUE: Many of the personal products that we use are causing harm to the environment because of the ingredients being used, how the product is being made, and the packaging of the product. In addition, many of these products are not produced with natural ingredients, and are instead made with many chemicals. Products are usally wrapped in plastic which is not good for the environment because plastic can relase harmful chemicals. They affect our environment by making its way through lakes, rivers, and oceans causing pollution which can hurt sea life and eventually us. It isn't healthy for us or the environment.

HOW IT'S HARMING THE ENVIRONMENT

EXCESSIVE WASTE: Every wonder what happens to the leftover soap bars and bottles that you don't use at the hotel? Well obviously, they get thrown out and...they end up in the landfill. However, when it ends up in the landfill, it causes harm to the environment. The shampoo bottles and soap bars often half used or barely used become waste right after they're thrown in the landfill. Because almost every bottle created with plastic, "95% of the plastic used in your hotel room end up in landfill where they sit for hundreds of years" according to Plastic waste is a huge problem and it's causing pollution in our environment. In addition to that, this causes higher garbarge removal costs for the hotel and makes environmental standards harder for hotels to meet.

UNNATURAL INGREDIENTS: A common ingredient found in many personal care products are microbreads. Microbeads are small, tiny plastic particles that are often used in personal care products. These particles are composed of polyethylene (PE) or polypropylene (PP), polyethylene terephthalate (PET), polymethlyl methacrylate (PMMA) or nylon. AKA plastic. Microbeads are used in in your body and face washes, but also in toothpaste, sunscreen, lip gloss, eyeliner, shampoo, deodorant, and soaps. Because these beads are so tiny, they can easily be washed down through sinks/bathtubs, soon polluting the ocean. Fish may accidently mistaken it as food and end up eating it.
